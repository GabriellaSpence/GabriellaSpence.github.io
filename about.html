About Me

I am a people analytics professional passionate about driving decisions with data and developing automation solutions. I have a background in Industrial-Organizational Psychology and found my home in the comfort of data and coding. I began this website to document my professional development journey in June 2022. Hopefully, as time goes by, you will find something interesting in the pages of my blog or the personal projects I present.

My Experience

City of Seattle: In my current role, I guide HR data and technology strategy for Seattle Department of Human Resources. I bridge communications between IT and business stakeholders. I have initial conversations to collect buisness requirements and then propose automated data solutions. I mostly leverage my power platform knowledge, SQL and R skills, and project management skills in this current role.

EY: I acted as a data consultant to inform HR decision-making in various companies. I worked with stakeholders to understand the data context, tell cohesive stories, and develop actionable insights. I analyzed large data sets using Power BI, R, and Excel. I generated reports and presented data to a broad range of audiences.

Evolent Health: For 2+ years, I served on a small Talent Analytics team as an analyst aligned to all sub-departments in the HR team (e.g., recruiting, L&D, HR Ops, etc.). I helped build the foundation of our data and reporting systems. My work included survey development and analysis, data validations and preprocessing, report automation, and dashboarding. Although my core focus was on operational and advanced reporting, there was time for occasional advanced analytics projects.
